How are we doing?


Current status shows the most recent figures we have;
usually the last full academic year.

Children whose first language is not English face a double hurdle when they start school; they need to learn everything their peers are learning, as well as learning a new language. Some may already be bilingual, with parents who speak both languages with them regularly, while others might speak almost no English at all due to a recent move.

While Devon is considered a monocultural society by many, the data we have on school pupils shows we actually have a very diverse population and diversity is growing steadily.
